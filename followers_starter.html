So, Why is Following Difficult?

The truth is, at intermediate level or above, it becomes mind-boggling how many skill layers a good follower has. However, many find that learning to follow as a beginner to be less overwhelming than learning to lead as a leader. I do want to emphasize one thing, which is the car analogy I used in my Leaders' Starter page. In older days and darker times, teachers refered to leaders as "drivers" and followers as "cars"; this analogy is simply incorrect. Aside from implying objectification and lowering follower's relative importance, the old version of the analogy teaches beginner followers the wrong mindset to succeed. Followers, you are driving your own "cars". You have the power to decide how to move on the dance floor. However, "with great power comes great responsibility", so this also demands more from the followers, but it's a good thing, trust me.

Good Following is a Mental State with Physical Conditioning

If learning to lead is about multi-tasking, then learning to follow is about preparing yourself mentally and physically. One of my most memorable amazing dances of all time happened with a day-1 follower (to be fair though, she has extensive background in another dance). During a guided practica, I danced with her in close embrace (already a rare feat for day-1 follower, but this also speaks very highly of how good the tango teacher of day is). It was going well, yet at one point, she let out a very audible sigh. I paused and broke away, wanting to ask her what was wrong. "What's wrong?", she beat me to the punch. "Oh I heard you sighing, so I want to check if everything is okay", I replied. "No no no it was amazing! It was a sigh of pleasure!" There is a word that describe this, but since you're already thinking it, I am not going to spell it out.

I often think about what makes that dance great. I mean, she couldn't do any ocho! I only had a 25% rate of success in tricking her to do a proper giro/molinete. I did come up with the below list:

  • She had impeccable balance. Even in closer embrace, she was not relying on me for support.
  • As the dance went on, I gradually gained her trust and she relaxed more and more, and then it's as if she understood my lead better, moved smoother, and heard the music the same way I heard it.
  • Unlike many beginner followers, she didn't *work too hard* and rush her steps.

When beginner followers verbally agree to accept my feedback, I find it *always* better to guide them towards improved balance, greater relaxation, and more patience. When these three traits, sometimes they can even figure out how to correctly do a move that they had not learned before. Of course, me being the weirdo that I am, I would say "hey you're doing great! I do have one weird suggestion though", then:

  • If that follower has poor balance in open embrace (usually due to poor postures), I would say "imagine I am a ghost and you're trying to mirror my movements on your own."
  • If that follower is tense and/or move erratically, I would say "hey, I want you to meet my right earlobe. That's my favorite earlobe. Try only looking at my right earlobe and stay in front of it."
  • If that follower is rushing and/or anticipating my lead, I would say "would it be possible for you to dance lazy? No need to work too hard."

These ridiculous suggestions are round-about ways to unload my followers' brains and guide them towards improved balance and greater relaxation. Now that you know my secrets, you might be pleased to know that, improved balance by engaging your core muscles is actually what allows those fast, flashy moves to happen; greater relaxation is a technique that one very advanced follower used on me to unshackle my creative side as a lead; patience makes your dance more smooth and graceful. Yeah, these three traits might just serve you for years to come.

These three traits directly influence the quality of your following, which I tend to define it as connection. As tango teacher Mira Barakat said, 

Tango is only possible when you and your partner have a connected embrace,
like how electrons can only flow in a complete circuit.
Someone should nominate her for a Nobel prize for this quote. All these preparation steps are to prepare followers' minds and bodies for a good connection.

Reasons I Enjoy Dancing with Beginner Followers

I very much enjoy dancing with beginner followers. Don't believe me? See reasons below:

  • Leading a beginner follower forces me to focus on musicality.
  • I, as a leader, will have off-days; I feel embarassed to lead intermediate/advanced followers on my off-days, but I still want to dance.
  • Since it is harder to lead a move correctly with a beginner follower, when it works, it is *much* more satisfying for both me and my follower.

Somewhat related, I actually knows a leader who only dances with beginner followers; I guess I am not the worst weirdo in town.
